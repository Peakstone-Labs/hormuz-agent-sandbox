# Scenario Background: Strait of Hormuz Crisis
## As of April 26, 2026 (Simulation Start Date)

### Timeline of Key Events

**Feb 15-20:** Iran tripled oil exports and drew down storage, pre-positioning for disruption.

**Feb 28:** The United States and Israel launched surprise joint airstrikes across Iran, killing Supreme Leader Ali Khamenei, Defense Minister Aziz Nasirzadeh, IRGC commander Mohammad Pakpour, and Defence Council secretary Ali Shamkhani, along with four top intelligence officials. Dozens of civilian casualties reported.

**Mar 1:** Iran launched retaliatory missile and drone strikes against Israel, US military bases in the region, and Gulf states (Bahrain, Kuwait, Qatar, Saudi Arabia, UAE). The IRGC declared the Strait of Hormuz closed, threatening to set fire to any ship attempting transit. The oil tanker *Skylight* was struck north of Khasab, Oman, killing two Indian crew members.

**Mar 4:** IRGC formally enforced the Strait closure. Shipping traffic collapsed from 100+ vessels/day to near zero.

**Mar 8:** Brent crude surpassed $100/barrel for the first time in four years. Mojtaba Khamenei (son of the killed Supreme Leader) was elected as new Supreme Leader; IRGC and senior officials pledged allegiance.

**Mar 9:** President Trump announced intent to seize control of the Strait of Hormuz and warned Iran against laying mines.

**Mar 12:** Iran had carried out 21 confirmed attacks on merchant ships.

**Mar 19:** US Armed Forces began a military campaign to forcibly open the Strait.

**Mar 25:** Iran rejected the US 15-point peace proposal. Iran presented 5 conditions: (1) end of US-Israeli attacks on Iran and pro-Iranian forces, (2) mechanisms to prevent war resumption, (3) compensation for damages, (4) international recognition of Iranian sovereignty over the Strait, (5) [undisclosed].

**Mar 26:** Israel killed Iran's top naval commander, Alireza Tangsiri.

**Mar 27:** Brent crude hit $114/barrel after ceasefire negotiations failed and IRGC re-declared the Strait closed. Peak price during crisis: $126/barrel.

**Apr 1:** Kuwait International Airport struck by Iranian drone fire, causing large fire at fuel storage tanks. Trump claimed Iran's president requested a ceasefire; Iran denied this. Iran stated: "We are not seeking a ceasefire, we are seeking to end the war."

**Apr 3:** An F-15E was shot down over Iran (pilot rescued, second crew member missing). A second US plane (A-10) was struck near the Strait (pilot ejected and rescued). Iran struck Gulf refineries. Trump threatened to destroy Iranian bridges, power plants, and desalination facilities. 40 nations convened (without US/Israel) to discuss diplomatic reopening of the Strait. France declared forcible reopening "unrealistic."

**Apr 7:** Less than two hours before Trump's deadline for "wide-scale destruction" of Iranian infrastructure, the US and Iran agreed to a **two-week ceasefire**, mediated by Pakistan. Trump suspended bombing on the condition that Iran reopens the Strait of Hormuz. Iran put forward a 10-point proposal that Trump called "a workable basis on which to negotiate."

**Apr 8:** Ceasefire formally took effect, but Israel launched **Operation Eternal Darkness** — 100 airstrikes on Lebanon in 10 minutes targeting Hezbollah assets. Kuwait reported 28 drone attacks; UAE reported 35 attacks the same day. A fire ignited at the Lavan Oil Refinery (attribution unclear). Iran began charging tolls of **over $1 million per ship** and only a handful of vessels transited the strait.

**Apr 9:** Only 4 dry cargo ships crossed the strait the first day (vs 9 during wartime). Hezbollah claimed responsibility for rocket attacks on northern Israel and pledged to continue if Israeli strikes did not stop. Iranian Parliamentary Speaker Mohammad Bagher Ghalibaf warned "time is running out" for the ceasefire.

**Apr 10:** Israeli strikes on Lebanon continued. Hezbollah fired rockets at Metula; sirens sounded in Tel Aviv, Haifa, and Ashdod. Trump publicly accused Iran of doing "a very poor job" on Hormuz, citing only 15 ships transited since Apr 8.

**Apr 11-12:** **Islamabad Talks.** US delegation of ~300 led by VP JD Vance, with envoys Steve Witkoff and Jared Kushner. Iran's ~70-member delegation led by Parliamentary Speaker Ghalibaf with FM Abbas Araghchi. Pakistan mediating (PM Sharif, FM Ishaq Dar, Field Marshal Asim Munir). After **21 hours of negotiation, both delegations left without a deal**. Main sticking points: Iran's nuclear program, Hormuz status, sanctions relief, and **$6B in frozen Iranian assets** (US offered phased relief tied to compliance; Iran demanded comprehensive lifting).

**Apr 13:** Trump declared and implemented a **full US naval blockade of Iran**, interdicting vessels entering or departing Iranian ports while permitting non-Iranian-bound transit. US Navy destroyers entered the Strait of Hormuz for mine-clearance operations — first US transit since the war began.

**Apr 14:** Iranian drones struck an exiled Kurdish militant camp in Iraqi Kurdistan, injuring three.

**Apr 16:** Iraqi Kurdistan reported a cumulative 16 missile/drone attacks since the ceasefire began.

**Apr 17:** Citing the Israel-Lebanon truce, Iran announced the Strait would be open to commercial shipping. The US blockade continued; Iran responded by re-imposing restrictions within 24 hours.

**Apr 18:** Six cruise ships successfully evacuated the Persian Gulf during a brief window when both sides claimed the strait was open.

**Apr 19:** US forces attacked and seized the Iran-flagged cargo ship **Touska** in the Gulf of Oman, disabling its engine room after a six-hour warning period. The US Navy also intercepted Iranian-flagged tankers *Deep Sea*, *Dorena*, *Sevin*, and *Derya* in Indian Ocean operations during this period.

**Apr 20:** Strait traffic collapsed back to March levels. The IMO reported (Apr 21) approximately **20,000 mariners and 2,000 ships stranded** in the region.

**Apr 21:** Trump announced an **indefinite extension of the ceasefire** at Pakistan's request — to give Iran time to submit a unified proposal — while explicitly keeping the naval blockade in place. Iran's senior adviser to Speaker Ghalibaf publicly dismissed the extension as **"meaningless"** while the blockade remains. VP Vance's planned second-round trip was called off.

**Apr 22:** Trump gave Iran **three to five days** to resolve internal government conflicts before resuming attacks. Iran seized container ships **Epaminondas** and **MSC Francesca** after gunfire and RPG attacks despite prior Iranian clearance. An Israeli strike in Lebanon killed journalist Amal Khalil — the 8th journalist killed by Israel in Lebanon in two months.

**Apr 23:** Brent crude rose ~3% to **$105.07/bbl** as talks remained stalled. Trump said publicly he "doesn't want to be rushed" to end the war.

**Apr 24:** Trump announced from the White House a **3-week extension of the Israel-Lebanon ceasefire** with Lebanese and Israeli ambassadors present (Hezbollah not party to talks). Israel resumed strikes on southern Lebanon within hours, claiming to have killed 6 Hezbollah fighters in Bint Jbeil. Hezbollah's Ali Fayyad called the extension "meaningless." Brent settled at **~$105.30/bbl** — capping a **~16-18% weekly gain**. The White House said envoys Witkoff and Kushner would travel to Islamabad on Saturday for direct talks.

**Apr 25:** Trump abruptly **canceled Witkoff and Kushner's trip to Islamabad**, saying Iran "offered a lot, but not enough." Iran responded by **attacking 3 commercial ships and seizing 2** of them, calling the US naval blockade the "main obstacle" to new peace talks. Iranian FM Araghchi met Pakistan's Army Chief Munir in Islamabad — but with no US counterpart, the negotiation track is effectively suspended.

**Apr 26 (Today):** The Apr 7 ceasefire is technically still in indefinite extension, but operationally degraded:
- Direct US-Iran talks are suspended after Trump's Apr 25 cancellation
- The US naval blockade remains; Iran continues to seize commercial vessels in retaliation
- Israel-Lebanon ceasefire was nominally extended Apr 24 but is being violated daily
- Trump's "3-5 day" Apr 22 ultimatum has effectively expired with no Iranian unified proposal
- Brent above $105, having gained ~16-18% on the week as the diplomatic track collapses
- Hormuz traffic at March-war lows; ~20,000 mariners and 2,000 ships still stranded

The next several days are the operative window: either Iran submits a proposal acceptable enough to restart negotiations, or Trump returns to the "wide-scale destruction" posture he held on Apr 7.

- **Production shut in:** ~13 million bpd of Middle East output offline due to inability to export through Hormuz. Saudi **East-West Pipeline restored to full ~7M bpd** capacity on Apr 12-13 after earlier strike damage; Yanbu Red Sea exports running ~5M bpd crude + ~0.9M bpd refined products (terminal-limited, not pipeline-limited).
- **Market read:** Prices are pricing in **collapse, not resolution** — a credible restart of negotiations could pull Brent back toward $90, but a resumption of US strikes (Trump's "wide-scale destruction" threat) would push Brent through $120 again.

### OPEC+ Status
- **Apr 5 OPEC+ meeting** agreed to a modest output adjustment of **+206 kbpd** from the 1.65 mbpd voluntary cut tranche — but the increase is **largely "on paper"** as Iraq/Kuwait/UAE physically cannot deliver through Hormuz
- Spare capacity ~5 mb/d nominal: **Saudi ~3 mb/d, UAE ~1 mb/d, Kuwait ~0.4 mb/d**, Iraq limited — but the buffer is stranded behind the chokepoint
- **Iraq is ~95% Hormuz-dependent** (~4 mb/d normal exports) — most exposed OPEC member
- Saudi Arabia is the only major producer with a working bypass: **East-West Pipeline at full ~7 mb/d capacity** since Apr 12-13; Yanbu Red Sea terminals are now the throughput constraint (~3-5 mb/d effective)
- Consensus view: Brent stays above $100 throughout 2026 if Hormuz disruption extends another month
