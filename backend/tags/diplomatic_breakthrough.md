# Diplomatic Breakthrough

The 40-nation coalition (excluding US/Israel) that convened on April 3 represents a significant diplomatic force. Led by Britain and France, with UAE participation, this group has the collective economic and political weight to propose a credible ceasefire framework — even over US objections.

## Injection

- **Iran** receives a face-saving off-ramp: the coalition proposes international oversight of Strait navigation in exchange for ceasefire, sanctions relief, and security guarantees. Accepting would end the immediate military threat but require abandoning the blockade leverage.
- **US** faces diplomatic isolation if the coalition reaches a deal without American input. Trump must decide whether to join a multilateral framework he didn't design or risk being the obstacle to peace.
- **Israel** worries that any diplomatic deal will leave Iran's nuclear program intact. May attempt to sabotage negotiations through unilateral military action.
- **Gulf States** see the coalition as their best path — they can broker between the 40-nation group and Washington.
- **Market** should price in de-escalation probability — even rumors of progress can drop oil $5-10/bbl.
