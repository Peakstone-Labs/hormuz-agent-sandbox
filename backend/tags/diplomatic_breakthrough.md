# Diplomatic Breakthrough

The Apr 11-12 Islamabad Talks failed after 21 hours; Trump canceled the second round on Apr 25. But Pakistan continues to mediate, and Saudi Arabia + UAE have been actively pushing for a **wider regional summit** (potentially with China underwriting) — a framework Trump has not endorsed but has not rejected either. UK is publicly aligned with the Saudi proposal; France/EU back the diplomatic track.

This tag triggers when one of two things happens: (a) Iran's internal factions converge on a unified proposal Trump can accept as a "workable basis," restarting the Islamabad track; or (b) the wider regional summit is formally convened with Saudi/UAE/Pakistan/China as conveners.

## Injection

- **Iran** receives a face-saving off-ramp: a phased deal trading controlled-passage protocol + partial sanctions relief + further frozen-asset release for ending ship seizures and accepting third-party Strait monitoring. Accepting requires the IRGC hardliners to be overruled by Mojtaba Khamenei.
- **US** must decide whether to lift the naval blockade — Iran's stated precondition. Trump can either claim a "win" for getting Iran to the table or face accusations of weakness from the Israel-aligned wing of his coalition. Brent dropping to $90 would relieve domestic political pressure significantly.
- **Israel** treats any deal that leaves the nuclear program intact as catastrophic. May escalate Lebanon strikes, conduct unilateral strikes on Iranian assets in Syria/Iraq, or leak intelligence to sabotage negotiations.
- **Gulf States** are the natural brokers — Saudi-led wider regional summit is their best diplomatic upside; they can extract security guarantees and post-war commitments from Washington.
- **Market** should price in de-escalation probability aggressively — credible talks restart pulls Brent toward $90 within a week. Even rumor of a summit announcement drops Brent $5-10/bbl. Implied vol collapses; war-risk insurance premiums begin to normalize.
