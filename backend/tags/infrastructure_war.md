# Infrastructure War

Trump has threatened to destroy Iranian bridges, power plants, and desalination facilities. Iran has counter-threatened to destroy Gulf energy and water infrastructure if its civilian systems are targeted. This represents a catastrophic escalation from military to civilian targeting.

## Injection

- **US** may follow through on Trump's threat to strike Iranian civilian infrastructure (power grid, desalination, bridges) to break Iranian will. This is effective but carries enormous humanitarian and diplomatic costs.
- **Iran** has promised symmetric retaliation against Gulf state infrastructure — Aramco facilities, ADNOC refineries, desalination plants that Gulf populations depend on for drinking water.
- **Gulf States** face an existential infrastructure threat. A successful Iranian strike on Saudi desalination could create a humanitarian crisis affecting millions.
- **Market** should price in supply destruction (not just disruption). Damaged refineries and export terminals take months to repair, creating a structural supply deficit.
