# Nuclear Brinkmanship

Iran's nuclear program was already at 60% enrichment before the war. Under extreme military pressure and with its conventional deterrent degraded, Iran may calculate that a rapid sprint to 90% weapons-grade uranium is its only guarantee against regime change.

## Injection

- **Iran** may announce or covertly pursue a nuclear breakout (enrichment to 90%) as the ultimate deterrent. This is a high-risk, high-reward move — it could deter further strikes or trigger an even more devastating response.
- **Israel** treats this as the ultimate red line. Any credible intelligence of a nuclear breakout would trigger immediate strikes on Natanz, Fordow, and Isfahan — potentially with bunker-busting munitions.
- **US** faces a dilemma: escalating to prevent nuclear breakout risks a wider war, but allowing it creates an existential threat to allies.
- **Market** should price in nuclear tail risk — gold spikes, oil becomes secondary to existential geopolitical fear.
