# China Steps In

China brokered the Saudi-Iran rapprochement in March 2023. It is Iran's largest oil customer and holds significant economic leverage. With the US-China trade war ongoing (36% average tariff), China has strategic interest in both stabilizing energy prices and embarrassing Washington diplomatically.

## Injection

- **Iran** receives a backchannel offer from Beijing: China will guarantee Iran's oil exports and provide economic lifeline in exchange for partial Strait reopening to Chinese-flagged vessels. This creates a face-saving de-escalation path.
- **US** faces a dilemma: a China-brokered deal would be a massive diplomatic humiliation, but rejecting it prolongs the crisis and economic pain.
- **Gulf States** may welcome Chinese mediation as an alternative to being caught in the US-Iran crossfire. Saudi Arabia has existing diplomatic channels with Beijing.
- **Market** should price in the possibility of partial Strait reopening — a controlled de-escalation that could bring oil down 10-15%.
