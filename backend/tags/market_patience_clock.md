# Market Patience Clock

**"No major crisis ever looks like a major crisis on its first day."** Markets initially give crises about 3 weeks of patience — one month of inventory, most can tolerate. But we are now in week 5.

The dangerous consensus was: "If things can be resolved in 2-3 weeks, everything is fine." Many institutions set the conflict-downgrade deadline at end of March. That deadline has passed without resolution. The 2-3 week resolution window that US investors still clung to is now clearly broken.

Historical pattern: Wars rarely announce ceasefire dates at the start. People thought Russia-Ukraine would end in hours, then days, then weeks — it lasted years. The Vietnam War followed the same arc.

## Injection

- **US** faces mounting economic pressure. Oil above $100 creates stagflation risk. Every day the Strait stays closed, the market narrative shifts from "temporary disruption" to "structural supply crisis." The futures curve in violent backwardation signals physical tightness. Gas prices above $5/gallon are politically toxic with midterms approaching.
- **Gulf States** watch their GDP growth collapse from expected 3-4% to potentially negative. Capital flight accelerates as the crisis extends. Their 3M bbl/day spare capacity becomes more valuable but also harder to deploy without security guarantees.
- **Market** should model patience as a depleting resource. After week 3, each additional week without resolution causes disproportionate price moves. Institutional models break. Supply chain cascading effects begin (insurance pullouts, tanker rerouting, refinery input shortages). Even if the crisis resolves, prices won't return to pre-crisis levels due to inventory rebuilding and permanently higher risk premiums.
