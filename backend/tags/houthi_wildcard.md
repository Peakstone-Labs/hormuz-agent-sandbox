# Houthi Wildcard

Yemen's Ansar Allah (Houthi) forces have declared the Red Sea a combat zone in coordination with Iran. They possess anti-ship missiles (C-802 variants), explosive-laden USVs, and naval mines capable of disrupting the Bab el-Mandeb strait.

## Injection

- **Iran** gains the option to activate Houthi attacks on Saudi Red Sea oil exports through Yanbu (~4M bbl/day). This is a second-strike capability that could shut down the only alternative to Hormuz.
- **Gulf States** must factor in the existential threat to the Yanbu corridor. Saudi Arabia's Red Sea ports become high-risk.
- **Market** should price in the risk of a dual-strait blockade (Hormuz + Bab el-Mandeb), which would remove ~25% of global seaborne oil from the market.
