# Hezbollah Front Escalation

The Lebanon front is **already active** — Israel's Apr 8 *Operation Eternal Darkness* (100 strikes in 10 minutes) reopened it within hours of the US-Iran ceasefire taking effect. Hezbollah's Apr 24 rocket barrage killed 11 Israelis. The Apr 24 3-week ceasefire extension between Israel and Lebanon is being violated daily by both sides; ~2,300 Lebanese killed, ~1.2M displaced.

This tag triggers a **further escalation** that breaks the carve-out and pulls the rest of the war back in: a major Hezbollah missile barrage on Tel Aviv, an Israeli ground incursion, or Hezbollah leadership formally declaring full war.

## Injection

- **Israel** must now fight on two fronts simultaneously. Hezbollah's residual rocket arsenal — even degraded — can saturate Iron Dome over population centers. A ground operation into southern Lebanon becomes politically inevitable, draining resources from the Iran posture.
- **Iran** sees the Lebanon front escalation as confirmation that the carve-out was a trap. Tehran gains a propaganda win but risks the IRGC pushing to formally exit the Apr 7 ceasefire — collapsing the diplomatic track entirely.
- **US** faces an impossible choice: join Israel in Lebanon (stretches forces already committed to the Iran blockade and three carrier strike groups), restrain Israel (impossible with current Trump-Netanyahu friction), or watch the Apr 7 deal die.
- **Gulf States** treat this as a worst-case path-dependent collapse — the wider regional summit they were pushing for becomes harder to convene.
- **Market** treats this as a major escalation. Brent snaps back through $115 within a session; the speculative call-skew on $130 strikes blows out.
