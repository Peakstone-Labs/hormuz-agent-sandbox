# Cyber Offensive

All parties possess significant cyber capabilities. Iran's APT groups (APT33/Elfin, APT34/OilRig) have previously targeted Gulf oil infrastructure. The US and Israel co-developed Stuxnet. In a hot war, cyber operations become force multipliers that can disable air defenses, disrupt communications, or sabotage industrial control systems.

## Injection

- **Iran** may launch cyberattacks against Gulf oil infrastructure SCADA systems, US military communications, or Israeli air defense networks as an asymmetric escalation tool.
- **US/Israel** may use cyber operations to disable Iranian air defenses, disrupt IRGC command-and-control, or sabotage the nuclear enrichment centrifuges (Stuxnet 2.0).
- **Gulf States** are vulnerable to Iranian cyber operations targeting Aramco, ADNOC, and desalination plant control systems — a repeat of the 2012 Shamoon attack at industrial scale.
- **Market** should factor in the risk of "invisible" supply disruptions — cyberattacks on oil infrastructure may not make headlines immediately but can take systems offline for weeks.
