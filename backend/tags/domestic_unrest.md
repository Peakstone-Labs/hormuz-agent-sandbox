# Domestic Unrest

War creates domestic political dynamics that constrain leaders' freedom of action. In Iran, the 2022-2023 Mahsa Amini protests showed deep public discontent — war may rally nationalist sentiment or reignite anti-regime protests. In the US, rising gas prices and military casualties create electoral pressure.

## Injection

- **Iran** faces a dual domestic challenge: (1) nationalist rallying effect that supports the regime, BUT (2) if infrastructure destruction causes water/power shortages, public anger could turn against the IRGC. Mojtaba Khamenei's legitimacy is not yet established — he is vulnerable to internal power struggles.
- **US** faces midterm political pressure. Gas prices above $5/gallon are politically toxic. Military casualties generate anti-war sentiment. Trump needs a "win" narrative — either decisive military victory or a deal he can brand as his achievement.
- **Market** should factor in the possibility of sudden policy reversals driven by domestic politics rather than strategic logic.
